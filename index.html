§ The Thesis

Legibility is the load-bearing concept.

Legibility is the ability to hold an accurate read of a situation while the situation actively works to destroy that read — under degraded communications, contested authority, and machine-tempo action faster than human review.

It is not transparency, and not documentation. A logging system documents after the fact; a legible system shows in the moment, in the artifact itself, inspectable in flight. The doctrine pages name a legibility problem; the codex pages render the answer.

§ The Form

The form is the enforcement surface.

Every folio obeys one fixed architecture. The discipline is not decorative — it makes the argument-to-artifact bind auditable. A reader holding any single folio can verify, in one glance, that the codex obeys the doctrine.

Recto · Doctrine
The argument. A named legibility problem in some domain.
Verso · Codex
The worked artifact. The argument rendered in operational form.

One mark per folio

A single color, used exclusively at the one operational moment doctrine and codex agree on. Decorative use is forbidden.

Three palette strata

Each folio carries 3 strata × 3–4 colors, named for its content register. Variation lives here, where it carries meaning.

Locked foundation

System core colors, paper spectrum, and typography are immutable across the series. EB Garamond · JetBrains Mono · Fraunces.

Auditable accessibility

Every body-text color meets WCAG 2.1 AA against its paper. The audit runs before render.
