FOLIO R · RECTO DOCTRINE PRESERVATION
Doctrine · Read on Retention

The truth you keep but cannot command.

FMFRP 12-80 and the preservation of unsanitizable doctrine.
Thesis

An institution that has become answerable — to law, to politics, to the rules of engagement — eventually cannot mandate the most brutal truths it still knows to be true. The Marine Corps met this limit in close combat. By 1991 it could not make Colonel Rex Applegate's maiming-first methods binding doctrine; so it did the next thing, and preserved them as a reference — retained without being commanded. The last-resort truth, kept legible precisely by refusing to bind it.

IThe sanitization problem

As a force formalizes, its official doctrine passes through legal review, the Rules of Engagement, and the Law of Armed Conflict. The rawest survival knowledge — point-blank lethality, permanent disablement, instinct ahead of technique — becomes impossible to codify as peacetime procedure. The hazard is not that the knowledge is wrong. It is that the filter deletes it, and the deletion stays invisible until the hour it is needed.

IIThree readings of the maneuver

Reference, not manual. FMFRP 12-80 is a reference publication, explicitly “not intended to become doctrine.” The category is the entire move: hold the institutional memory without legally binding the force to it.

The unfilterable core. Applegate's thesis was psychological before it was physical — strip the conditioning of fair play, bypass cognition under autonomic stress, commit to overwhelming pre-emptive violence. No continuum of force can hold this part, and it is the part most fatal to lose.

Legibility under collapse. Target-focused point shooting over sighted fire inside ten feet; gross motor over fine; the body reverting to an “animal” state in seconds. The method is a way to keep an accurate read of a lethal moment when physiology is destroying your ability to read it.

IIIWhat preservation requires

  1. Retain, do not mandate. Keep the truth available; never command it. The category carries the legitimacy the content cannot.
  2. Preserve the unsanitized text. A filtered record is a lie of omission. The value lives in the unflinching original, not the version cleared for review.
  3. Name the custody. Attribute the chain — Shanghai, to the OSS, to 1943, to 1976, to the 1991 reprint. Provenance is what makes a retained truth trustworthy.

IVCoda

When technology fails, distance closes, and the rules of engagement evaporate, survival falls back on the primitive capacities the manual catalogs. The institution cannot order that fall-back into being. It can only refuse to let the knowledge of it disappear. That refusal — retain, attribute, do not command — is itself the doctrine.
